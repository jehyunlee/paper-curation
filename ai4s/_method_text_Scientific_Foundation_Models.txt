## Category Timeline: Scientific Foundation Models (2018–2026)

### SUB-THEME A: Foundation Model Genesis & Scientific Text Adaptation (2018→2024, stable)
Branches into:
- **"General Purpose Language Models"** (2018–2018): The foundational architecture exemplified by BERT, which established pre-training and bidirectional contextual understanding.
- **"Domain-Specific Language Models"** (2024–2024): The initial adaptation of general LLM architectures to scientific corpora, as seen with SciBERT and Galactica, enhancing performance on scientific text.
Interaction: This sub-theme **FEEDS INTO** all other sub-themes by providing the core architectural and methodological paradigms. It **ENABLES** the development of enhanced capabilities and domain-specific applications.

### SUB-THEME B: Enhancing Foundation Model Capabilities (2023→2026, growing)
Branches into:
- **"Tool Integration & Augmentation"** (2023–2024): Focus on empowering language models with external functionalities, exemplified by Toolformer learning to use calculators and search engines, and the development of Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) for knowledge-intensive tasks.
- **"Discovery Beyond Training Data"** (2026–2026): Methods to overcome the limitations of pre-trained models by expanding discovery spaces beyond narrow training data, as seen with Verifier-Constrained Flow Expansion.
Interaction: This sub-theme **RESPONDS TO** the inherent limitations of pure language modeling and **ENABLES** more reliable, accurate, and precise domain-specific applications. It **FEEDS INTO** the methodologies for scientific discovery.

### SUB-THEME C: Domain-Specific Scientific Applications (2024→2025, growing)
Branches into:
- **"Chemistry & Materials Science"** (2024–2025): Application of foundation models for tasks like materials discovery, property prediction, and synthesis, discussed in "A Perspective on Foundation Models in Chemistry" and "Foundation Models for Materials Discovery" (both 2024 and 2025 papers).
- **"Environmental Science"** (2025–2025): Exploration of foundation models for forward prediction, data generation, data assimilation, and downscaling in environmental contexts, as surveyed in "Foundation Models for Environmental Science".
- **"General Scientific Research Acceleration"** (2024–2024): Demonstrations of how foundation models, such as Gemini, can accelerate various scientific research workflows through case studies.
Interaction: This sub-theme **CONSUMES** foundational models from Sub-theme A and leverages enhanced capabilities from Sub-theme B. It **INFORMS** the research directions and evaluation criteria discussed in Survey & Future Outlook.

### SUB-THEME D: Survey, Conceptualization & Future Outlook (2024→2025, growing)
Branches into:
- **"Early Landscape & Cross-Domain Surveys"** (2024–2024): Initial comprehensive reviews mapping the nascent field of scientific foundation models, including papers like "Generative AI and the Foundation Model Era," "AI4Research," "LLM4SR," and surveys on "Scientific Large Language Models" focusing on biological, chemical, cross-field, and cross-modal applications.
- **"Data-Centric & Methodological Reviews"** (2025–2025): Deeper dives into the data foundations, core techniques, and evaluation practices for Sci-LLMs, as presented in "A Survey of Scientific Large Language Models: From Data Foundations to" and "Transforming Science with Large Language Models".
- **"Strategic Roadmaps for AI in Science"** (2024–2025): Visionary papers outlining the future trajectory and potential of AI for science, such as "Artificial Intelligence for Science — Bridging Data to Wisdom" and "AI for Science 2025".
Interaction: This sub-theme **REFLECTS ON** and **SYNTHESIZES** developments across all other sub-themes. It **SPAWNS** new research directions and **GUIDES** future efforts in scientific FM development.

### SUB-THEME E: Scalability & Computational Infrastructure (2024→2026, growing)
Branches into:
- **"Distributed Learning for Scientific FMs"** (2024–2024): Addressing the computational demands of large models through distributed approaches like Scalable Cross-Facility Federated Learning for Scientific Foundation Models.
- **"High-Performance Computing for LLMs"** (2026–2026): Focus on advanced parallelism techniques and GPU engineering for efficiently training and deploying large language models to enable scientific discovery, as explored in "From GPU Engineering to Scientific Discovery".
Interaction: This sub-theme **ENABLES** the growth and practical deployment of all other sub-themes by addressing the significant computational and data privacy demands of large models. It **SUPPORTS** the scaling of foundational models and their applications.

### CROSS-CUTTING CONNECTIONS
- Foundation Model Genesis & Scientific Text Adaptation (A) **FEEDS INTO** all other sub-themes by providing the core models and architectures.
- Enhancing Foundation Model Capabilities (B) **ENABLES** more effective Domain-Specific Scientific Applications (C) and supports advanced methods for Discovery Beyond Data.
- Scalability & Computational Infrastructure (E) **SUPPORTS** the expansion and practical deployment of all aspects of Foundation Model Genesis (A), Enhancing Capabilities (B), and Domain-Specific Applications (C).
- Survey, Conceptualization & Future Outlook (D) **RESPONDS TO** and **SYNTHESIZES** progress from all other sub-themes, and in turn, **GUIDES** future research directions and resource allocation.

### KEY MILESTONES (annotation boxes)
- **2018: BERT's Genesis** — The introduction of BERT established the pre-training paradigm for deep bidirectional transformers, fundamentally shaping the future of large language models.
- **2023: Tool Integration Emerges** — Toolformer demonstrates that language models can autonomously learn to use external tools, significantly expanding their utility and accuracy for complex tasks.
- **2024: Scientific FM Proliferation** — A critical mass of domain-specific scientific foundation models (e.g., SciBERT, Galactica) and comprehensive surveys emerge, solidifying Scientific Foundation Models as a distinct research area.
- **2026: Discovery Beyond Data Focus** — Research shifts towards overcoming inherent data limitations for true scientific discovery, exemplified by Verifier-Constrained Flow Expansion.
