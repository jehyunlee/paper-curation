## Category Timeline: Physical Sciences & Engineering (2017–2026)

### SUB-THEME A: Physics-Informed AI & Scientific Machine Learning (2017→2026, growing)
Branches into:
-   **"Foundations of Physics-Informed Neural Networks"** (2017–2024): This foundational period introduced and extensively reviewed Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) as a method for solving supervised learning tasks by embedding physical laws. Key papers include the seminal "Physics Informed Deep Learning (Part I)" (2017), "Scientific Machine Learning through Physics-Informed Neural Networks" (2022), and "Physics-Informed Neural Networks and Extensions" (2024). This also encompasses broader scientific machine learning principles, such as "Uncertainty quantification in scientific machine learning" (2024) and "The frontier of simulation-based inference" (2024).
-   **"Neural Operators for Parametric Systems"** (2025–2026): This sub-sub-theme focuses on neural operators, a powerful paradigm for approximating solutions to parametric Partial Differential Equations (PDEs) in infinite-dimensional function spaces. It includes comprehensive analyses like "Architectures, variants, and performance of neural operators" (2025) and "From Theory to Application: A Practical Introduction to Neural Operators" (2025). Advanced developments include "Neural-POD" (2026) for learning non-linear orthogonal bases, "SPINONet" (2026) integrating energy-efficient spiking neural networks, and applications such as "Physics-Informed Neural Operator for Electromagnetic Inverse Scatterin" (2026).
-   **"Enhancing PINN Robustness and Applicability"** (2026): This specific area refines PINN methodologies to overcome limitations and expand utility, addressing issues like generalization performance with "Incorporating Continuous Dependence Qualifies Physics-Informed Neural" (2026) and applying PINNs for "multi-objective design optimizatio" (2026) in complex engineering scenarios.
Interaction: This sub-theme **FEEDS INTO** "AI-Accelerated Materials & Chemical Discovery" by providing robust surrogate models and simulation acceleration techniques. It also **ENABLES** "Computational Science & Simulation at Scale" by offering more efficient numerical methods for complex physical systems.

### SUB-THEME B: AI-Accelerated Materials & Chemical Discovery (2023→2026, growing)
Branches into:
-   **"Graph Neural Networks for Materials Design"** (2023–2024): This early phase showcases the profound impact of graph neural networks (GNNs) for high-throughput materials discovery. A seminal contribution is "Scaling Deep Learning for Materials Discovery" (2023), which utilized GNoME with active learning to discover an unprecedented number of new stable crystal structures. This period also saw initial surveys on "AI for Materials Science: Foundation Models, LLM Agents, D" (2024) highlighting emerging AI paradigms.
-   **"Predictive Models for Molecular Dynamics & Properties"** (2025–2026): This area advances the development of machine-learned interatomic potentials and neural networks to directly predict molecular dynamics trajectories and physicochemical properties. "Artificial Intelligence for Direct Prediction of Molecular Dynamics Ac" (2025) introduced MDtrajNet and its foundational model MDtrajNet-1. Subsequent works include "DMFlow" (2026) for generating disordered crystal structures, "Machine-Learned Interatomic Potentials for Predicting Physicochemical" (2026) using Moment Tensor Potentials (MTP), and "General-Purpose Machine-Learned Potential for CrCoNi Alloys" (2026).
Interaction: This sub-theme **BRANCHES INTO** "Autonomous Science with LLM Agents" as LLMs become integrated into the materials design pipeline. It also **FEEDS INTO** "Computational Science & Simulation at Scale" by generating more accurate and efficient simulation inputs and models.

### SUB-THEME C: Autonomous Science with LLM Agents (2024→2026, rapidly growing)
Branches into:
-   **"LLMs for Knowledge Extraction & Domain Adaptation"** (2024–2025): This initial thrust harnesses Large Language Models (LLMs) to extract, synthesize, and adapt scientific knowledge. Papers such as "Knowledge-guided Large Language Model for Material Science" (2024) and "Large Language Model in Materials Science: Roles, Challenges, and Stra" (2024) discuss their application. "Fine-tuning Large Language Models for Domain Adaptation" (2024) explores model refinement. Later, "Harnessing Large Language Models to Collect and Analyze Metal-Organic" (2025) through L2M3 demonstrated automated data extraction from MOF literature, and "MatterChat" (2025) introduced a multi-modal LLM for material science by integrating atomic structure data.
-   **"Multi-Agent Systems for Workflow Automation"** (2024–2026): This rapidly expanding area focuses on designing collaborative AI agent systems to automate complex scientific workflows. Notable examples include **VASPilot** (2024) for autonomous DFT simulations, "MechAgents" (2024) for solving mechanics problems, "MatPilot" (2024) as an LLM-enabled AI materials scientist, and "OpenFOAMGPT 2.0" (2024) for computational fluid dynamics. The trend extends to "An automatic end-to-end chemical synthesis development platform" (2024), "Automating Computational Chemistry Workflows via OpenClaw and Domain-S" (2026), and "LLM-ODE" (2026) for data-driven discovery of dynamical systems.
Interaction: This sub-theme **SPAWNS** from general advancements in large language models. It **ENABLES** "Computational Science & Simulation at Scale" by automating complex simulation setups and analysis. It **MERGES WITH** aspects of "AI-Accelerated Materials & Chemical Discovery" by providing autonomous platforms for design and experimentation.

### SUB-THEME D: Computational Science & Simulation at Scale (2023→2026, growing)
Branches into:
-   **"Exascale Computing & Simulation Toolkits"** (2023–2024): This early phase acknowledges the growing importance of exascale computing and the development of specialized tools for large-scale simulations. "Simulations in the era of exascale computing" (2023) highlights the context, while "AMDAT" (2024) offers an open-source molecular dynamics analysis toolkit for supercomputers.
-   **"Vision for Autonomous Scientific Facilities"** (2026): This forward-looking area outlines the ambition for fully autonomous scientific infrastructure, from design to operation. "Toward a Fully Autonomous, AI-Native Particle Accelerator" (2026) presents a roadmap for self-driving accelerators. Similarly, "Polymer Brushes and Grafted Polymers: AI/ML-Driven Synthesis, Simulati" (2026) envisions "Self-Driving Labs" for polymer research, accelerating the entire experimental pipeline.
-   **"Autonomous PDE Solver Development"** (2026): This advanced development focuses on AI agents autonomously designing and implementing numerical solvers for partial differential equations. "AutoNumerics" (2026) proposes an LLM-based multi-agent pipeline that can independently design, implement, debug, and verify numerical solvers from natural language problem descriptions.
Interaction: This sub-theme **RESPONDS TO** the increasing complexity and scale of scientific challenges. It **IS ENABLED BY** advancements in "Physics-Informed AI & Scientific Machine Learning" for efficient numerical methods and "Autonomous Science with LLM Agents" for workflow automation and intelligent control.

### CROSS-CUTTING CONNECTIONS
-   The maturation of **Physics-Informed AI & Scientific Machine Learning** (PINNs, Neural Operators) provides foundational methods for building efficient and physically consistent models, which are then integrated into the autonomous workflows of **Autonomous Science with LLM Agents** and underpin the large-scale simulations within **Computational Science & Simulation at Scale**.
-   The data-driven insights and predictive models developed in **AI-Accelerated Materials & Chemical Discovery** are increasingly being utilized by **Autonomous Science with LLM Agents** to guide experimental design and accelerate the discovery process through integrated platforms like VASPilot and MatPilot.
-   The push towards **Computational Science & Simulation at Scale**, particularly with "Self-Driving Labs" and "AI-Native Particle Accelerators," is a grand vision that **MERGES** capabilities from all other sub-themes, requiring advanced AI models, automated workflows, and intelligent agent control for complex, real-world systems.
-   The rise of **Foundation Models** (highlighted in 2024 surveys, MatterChat 2025, MDtrajNet-1 2025) is a cross-cutting methodological advancement, acting as a powerful new type of AI that **ENABLES** rapid progress across **AI-Accelerated Materials & Chemical Discovery** and especially **Autonomous Science with LLM Agents** by providing versatile, pre-trained models.

### KEY MILESTONES (annotation boxes)
-   **2017: Emergence of Physics-Informed Neural Networks** — The introduction of PINNs in "Physics Informed Deep Learning (Part I)" marks a foundational shift towards integrating physical laws directly into deep learning models for scientific computation and solving PDEs.
-   **2023: Large-Scale Materials Discovery Acceleration** — The demonstration of GNoME in "Scaling Deep Learning for Materials Discovery" exemplifies AI's capacity for unprecedented acceleration in discovering new stable materials, multiplying known structures by an order of magnitude.
-   **2024: Proliferation of LLM Agents in Science** — A significant increase in papers (e.g., VASPilot, MatPilot, MechAgents, OpenFOAMGPT, OpenClaw) highlights the rapid adoption and development of large language model multi-agent systems for automating complex scientific workflows across materials, chemistry, and engineering.
-   **2026: Vision for Fully Autonomous Scientific Facilities** — Position papers like "Toward a Fully Autonomous, AI-Native Particle Accelerator" lay out ambitious roadmaps for AI to not just assist but autonomously design and operate complex scientific infrastructure, signifying a major long-term goal for AI for Science.
