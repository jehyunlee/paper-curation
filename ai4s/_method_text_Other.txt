## Category Timeline: Other

### SUB-THEME A: Mathematical Foundations & Structural Efficiency (2018→2024, CONVERGING)
Branches into:
- **"Continuous-Depth Modeling"** (2018–2021): Introduction of **Neural Ordinary Differential Equations** as a new family of deep learning models, shifting from discrete layers to continuous-time dynamics.
- **"Generalized Multi-view Clustering"** (2022–2023): Development of the **Align then Fusion** framework to solve large-scale anchor-based clustering through flexible structural alignment.
- **"Reliability & Uncertainty Quantification"** (2023–2024): Systematic categorization of **Uncertainty Quantification Methods** for DNNs, distinguishing between data and model uncertainty to improve safety.
- **"Efficient State-Space Inference"** (2024–2024): Transitioning to **State-Free Inference of State-Space Models** via the **Transfer Function Approach** to bypass memory bottlenecks in large state sizes.
Interaction: FEEDs into Sub-theme C by providing the mathematical reliability and computational efficiency required for autonomous agent logic.

### SUB-THEME B: Human-Centric Systems & Applied AI Workflows (2021→2025, DIVERGING)
Branches into:
- **"Social-Mobility Architecture"** (2021–2022): Designing **Architecture for Human-Driven Systems** that integrates social behavior into continuous engineering cycles.
- **"Semi-Supervised Vision"** (2023–2024): Advancing **2D Human Pose Estimation** using **Position Inconsistency** to leverage unlabeled data in human-centric spatial tasks.
- **"AI-Augmented Research"** (2023–2025): Practical integration of AI tools for hypothesis generation and literature review as outlined in the **AI for Research: Ultimate Guide**.
- **"Agentic Adoption Patterns"** (2024–2025): Empirical analysis of user behavior through the **Adoption and Usage of AI Agents** study, focusing on the **Perplexity Comet** browser.
Interaction: RESPONDs to the foundational shifts in Sub-theme A by applying them to real-world human environments and academic productivity.

### SUB-THEME C: Autonomous Agents & Cognitive Logic (2024→2025, EMERGING)
Branches into:
- **"Multi-Agent Supply Chains"** (2024–2025): Implementation of the **A2SC methodology** for **autonomous supply chains**, moving from manual logistics to multi-agent system (MAS) orchestration.
- **"Industrial MAS Case Studies"** (2024–2025): Validation of autonomous frameworks in specific sectors, such as the **autonomous meat supply chain** case study.
- **"Computational Reasoning"** (2025–2025): Formalization of **Reason** as a capacity for logic-based conclusion drawing from existing data within AI architectures.
- **"Logic-Driven Decision Making"** (2025–2025): Integration of conscious logic application into autonomous agents to ensure valid conclusions in complex environments.
Interaction: MERGEs the structural efficiency of SSMs from Sub-theme A with the user-centric insights of Sub-theme B to ENABLE fully autonomous industrial operations.

### CROSS-CUTTING CONNECTIONS
- Mathematical efficiency in **State-Space Models** directly enables the real-time processing required for **Autonomous Supply Chains**.
- **Uncertainty Quantification** frameworks provide the safety layer necessary for **Human-Driven Systems** and high-stakes **AI for Research**.
- The transition from **Neural ODEs** to **Reason**-based logic represents a shift from purely statistical patterns to structured, continuous, and logical inference.
