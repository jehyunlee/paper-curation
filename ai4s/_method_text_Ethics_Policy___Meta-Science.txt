## Category Timeline: Ethics, Policy & Meta-Science

### SUB-THEME A: AI Impact on Scientific Practice (2022→2026, DIVERGING)
Branches into:
- **"The Productivity-Diversity Paradox"** (2023–2026): Investigates the tension where AI tools expand individual scientist impact but contract the collective scope of inquiry, as evidenced by the analysis of 41.3 million papers showing a narrowing of the "scientific frontier."
- **"Cognitive Debt & Neuro-impact"** (2023–2025): Longitudinal neurophysiological studies using EEG and NLP to measure how LLM-assisted writing affects human brain connectivity and long-term cognitive functions.
- **"AI4Science Conceptual Frameworks"** (2022–2024): Establishing the "Data-to-Wisdom" hierarchy and the Dagstuhl 22382 agenda for bridging data-driven and mechanistic modeling.
- **"Social Science of AI"** (2024–2025): A dual-pathway survey classifying AI as both a tool for social science research and a subject of sociological study.
- **"Economic R&D Task Modeling"** (2023–2025): Theoretical frameworks modeling R&D as a set of heterogeneous tasks to evaluate AI’s impact on innovation economics.
- **"Domain-Specific Adoption Surveys"** (2023–2024): Large-scale usage tracking (e.g., Semantic Scholar authors, Harvard Medical School) to map how researchers integrate LLMs into workflows.
Interaction: **FEEDS** into Peer Review Automation by providing the volume of AI-generated content that necessitates automated oversight.

### SUB-THEME B: Peer Review Automation & Integrity (2023→2025, CONVERGING)
Branches into:
- **"LLM Detection & Forensics"** (2023–2025): Systematic investigation of AI-generated text in ICLR reviews and the development of 99%+ accuracy domain-specific detectors for admissions materials.
- **"NLP Problem Space Mapping"** (2024–2025): The Dagstuhl 24052 white paper effort to categorize every stage of the peer review process where NLP can contribute or intervene.
- **"Ethical Sentiment & Policy"** (2023–2024): Global surveys (e.g., Nature’s 5,000-researcher study) revealing deep divisions in the community regarding the legitimacy of AI in manuscript evaluation.
- **"Data Integrity Frameworks"** (2024–2026): Development of multi-faceted frameworks to combat the "integrity crisis" in fields like materials science caused by AI-accelerated data fabrication.
- **"Automated Admissions Screening"** (2023–2025): Specialized research into detecting AI-modified vs. AI-generated personal statements and recommendation letters.
Interaction: **RESPONDS** to the "Productivity Paradox" by attempting to filter the resulting surge in scientific output.

### SUB-THEME C: AI Agent Reliability & Human-AI Collaboration (2024→2026, EMERGING)
Branches into:
- **"Reliability Science for Agents"** (2024–2026): The transition from simple LLM tools to a formal "Science of AI Agent Reliability" focusing on predictable behavior in autonomous research agents.
- **"Human-AI Thought Partnership"** (2024–2026): Designing AI not as a tool for thought but as a "thought partner" capable of collaborative reasoning and learning.
- **"Labor Market & Agent Audits"** (2024–2025): Large-scale audits of 844 occupations using the O*NET database to distinguish between task automation and human augmentation.
- **"AI-Augmented Inventorship Law"** (2025–2026): Methodologies for validating human inventorship in AI-driven molecular discovery through "Audit Gates" for patent documentation.
- **"Security & Code Vulnerability"** (2023–2025): Empirical studies on OpenAI Codex and GitHub Copilot showing that AI assistants can lead developers to produce more insecure code.
Interaction: **SPAWNS** from the maturation of LLMs into agentic workflows, requiring new legal and safety standards.

### SUB-THEME D: Open Access & Publishing Reform (2015→2024, CONVERGING)
Branches into:
- **"OA Economic Feasibility"** (2015–2024): Long-term financial analysis proving that global subscription budgets are sufficient to cover a total transition to Open Access.
- **"Disciplinary Barriers Meta-synthesis"** (2023–2024): Analysis of 11 bibliometric studies identifying why OA adoption varies across academic disciplines.
- **"Search Engine Hegemony"** (2018–2024): Comparative studies of 12 academic databases (ASEBDs) showing Google Scholar’s increasing dominance over traditional citation indexes.
- **"Institutional Transition Models"** (2021–2024): Case studies of Norwegian SSH journals and Oxford University Press (OUP) reflecting the shift from subscription to OA.
Interaction: **ENABLES** the AI Impact theme by providing the massive, open-access datasets required for training AI4Science models.

### CROSS-CUTTING CONNECTIONS
- **Data Integrity ↔ AI Agents**: The rise of autonomous agents (Sub-theme C) creates new challenges for data integrity (Sub-theme B), as agents may inadvertently hallucinate or bypass human verification steps.
- **Open Access ↔ AI Impact**: The success of OA publishing (Sub-theme D) provides the raw material for the AI-driven expansion of scientific impact (Sub-theme A), but also facilitates the "contraction" of research diversity through algorithmic bias.

### KEY MILESTONES
- **2015: The OA Financial Proof** — Demonstration that the subscription model's total cost could fully fund a global Open Access ecosystem, triggering a decade of policy shifts.
- **2023: The Generative Shock** — Nature declares ChatGPT a "force for good and evil," marking the point where LLMs moved from niche tools to central scientific disruptors.
- **2025: AAAI Presidential Panel Report** — 24 leading experts and 475 community members formalize the future roadmap for AI research ethics and policy.

◀ **Subscription Publishing** absorbed into **Open Access Models** (**2024**)
