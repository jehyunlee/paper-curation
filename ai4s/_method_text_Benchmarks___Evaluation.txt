## Category Timeline: Benchmarks & Evaluation

### SUB-THEME A: Scientific Reasoning & Discovery Benchmarks (2023→2026, CONVERGING)
Branches into:
- **"Foundational Scientific Reasoning"** (2023–2024): Focuses on systematic examination of college-level problem-solving and reasoning capabilities using datasets like **SciBench**.
- **"Research-Grade Coding & Data Readiness"** (2024–2025): Transitions from general reasoning to domain-specific implementation, utilizing **SciCode** for scientist-curated coding problems and **SciHorizon** for assessing AI-for-Science data readiness.
- **"Trustworthiness & Rigorous Assessment"** (2024–2025): Establishes frameworks for evaluating the reliability of LLMs in scientific contexts through **SciTrust** and **ScienceAgentBench**, focusing on data-driven discovery automation.
- **"Scenario-Grounded Multi-Disciplinary Discovery"** (2025–2026): Culminates in comprehensive evaluations across biology, chemistry, and materials science as seen in **Evaluating Large Language Models in Scientific Discovery**.
Interaction: This sub-theme FEEDS into Agentic Environments by providing the ground-truth logic required for autonomous experimentation.

### SUB-THEME B: Autonomous Agent & Tool-Use Environments (2023→2026, ACCELERATING)
Branches into:
- **"Software Engineering & Issue Resolution"** (2023–2024): Initial focus on real-world problem solving in GitHub repositories using **SWE-bench**.
- **"Scientific Tool-Use Gymnasiums"** (2024–2025): Development of interactive environments like **Aviary** and **SciAgentGym** to benchmark multi-step tool manipulation in research settings.
- **"Stable Tool Learning & Scalability"** (2025–2025): Addressing API instability and evaluation randomness through **StableToolBench** and scaling to domain-specific coding in **MedAgentGym**.
- **"Long-Term Coherence & Autonomous Operations"** (2025–2026): Testing agent persistence in simulated environments like **Vending-Bench** and **AIRS-Bench**.
- **"End-to-End Research Autonomy"** (2026–2026): The emergence of **ResearchGym**, enabling full-cycle hypothesis generation, experimental design, and evaluation.
Interaction: This sub-theme SPAWNS from Software Engineering benchmarks and ENABLES the transition from passive evaluation to active research agents.

### SUB-THEME C: Human-AI Comparative Performance (2023→2025, STABILIZING)
Branches into:
- **"Annotation & Classification Efficiency"** (2023–2024): Comparing LLMs against crowd workers for text-annotation tasks, showing **ChatGPT** outperforming traditional human-in-the-loop methods.
- **"Complex Textual & Linguistic Analysis"** (2024–2024): Evaluating LLMs against outsourced human coders for specialized tasks like NLP-based news analysis.
- **"Creative & Divergent Thinking"** (2024–2025): Assessing the limits of AI in creative tasks like the **Alternative Uses Task**, where top-tier humans still maintain an edge over chatbots.
- **"Novelty in Research Idea Generation"** (2025–2025): Large-scale human studies comparing LLM-generated research ideas against expert NLP researchers to determine "blind" quality scores.
Interaction: This sub-theme RESPONDS to the benchmarks in Sub-theme A by providing a human-centric baseline for "novelty" and "creativity."

### SUB-THEME D: Specialized Automation & Utility Benchmarking (2024→2026, DIVERSIFYING)
Branches into:
- **"AutoML Framework Assessment"** (2024–2024): Practical evaluation of 16 **AutoML tools** (AutoGluon, TPOT, etc.) across diverse classification tasks.
- **"Academic Workflow Automation"** (2024–2025): Benchmarking AI as peer reviewers for survey paper relevance via **RelevAI-Reviewer**.
- **"Content Transformation & Accessibility"** (2025–2026): Evaluating the conversion of static research into interactive formats through **Paper2Web**.
Interaction: This sub-theme MERGES with Agentic Environments to provide specialized "skills" for the end-to-end research pipeline.

### CROSS-CUTTING CONNECTIONS
- **Agentic Integration**: The shift from static benchmarks (**SciBench**) to interactive environments (**ResearchGym**) represents a fundamental move toward evaluating "doing" rather than just "knowing."
- **Human-Baseline Calibration**: Comparative studies (Human vs. AI) are increasingly used to validate the "rigor" of new scientific benchmarks like **ScienceAgentBench**.
- **Stability in Evaluation**: The move from **ToolBench** to **StableToolBench** highlights a field-wide push for reproducible and reliable agent evaluation metrics.

### KEY MILESTONES
- **2023: The Software Engineering Pivot** — **SWE-bench** establishes that LLMs can be evaluated on real-world, multi-file GitHub issues, moving beyond isolated code snippets.
- **2024: The Scientific Tool-Use Explosion** — The release of **Aviary** and **SciAgentGym** marks the transition of LLM agents from general assistants to specialized scientific instruments.
- **2026: Full-Cycle Research Autonomy** — **ResearchGym** emerges as the first comprehensive environment for evaluating the entire scientific method (Hypothesis → Evaluation) within a single framework.

▶ Scientific Agentic Research emerges (2024)
◀ Human-only annotation absorbed into LLM-driven pipelines (2024)
