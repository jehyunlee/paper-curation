In this work, we present the first study on large language models (LLMs) as evolutionary combinatorial optimiz- ers.. The main advantage is that it requires minimal domain knowledge and human efforts, as well as no additional training of the model.. This approach is referred to as LLM-driven EA (LMEA).. Specifically, in each generation of the evolutionary search, LMEA instructs the LLM to select parent solutions from current population, and perform crossover and mutation to generate offspring solutions.. Then, LMEA evaluates these new solutions and include them into the population for the next generation.. LMEA is equipped with a self-adaptation mechanism that controls the temperature of the LLM..
