However, the inherent open-ended nature of these tasks implies that there is always room for improvement in the quality of model responses.. To address this challenge, various approaches have been proposed to enhance the performance of LLMs.. There has been a growing focus on enabling LLMs to self-improve their response quality, thereby reducing the reliance on extensive human annotation efforts for collecting diverse and high-quality train- ing data.. Recently, prompting-based methods have been widely explored among self-improvement methods owing to their effectiveness, efficiency, and conve- nience.. However, those methods usually require explicitly and thoroughly written rubrics as inputs to LLMs.. It is expensive and challenging to manually derive and provide all necessary rubrics with a real-world complex goal for improvement (e.g., being more helpful and less harmful).
