We study mixed-gender research teams, examining 6.6 million papers published across the medical sciences since 2000 and establishing several core findings.. First, the fraction of publications by mixed-gender teams has grown rapidly, yet mixed-gender teams continue to be underrepresented compared to the expectations of a null model.. Second, despite their underrepresentation, the publications of mixed- gender teams are substantially more novel and impactful than the publications of same- gender teams of equivalent size..
