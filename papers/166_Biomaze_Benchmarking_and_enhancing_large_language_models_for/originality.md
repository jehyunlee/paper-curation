ABSTRACT The applications of large language models (LLMs) in various biological do- mains have been explored recently, but their reasoning ability in complex bio- logical systems, such as pathways, remains underexplored, which is crucial for predicting biological phenomena, formulating hypotheses, and designing exper- iments.. This work explores the potential of LLMs in pathway reasoning.. We introduce BioMaze, a dataset with 5.1K complex pathway problems derived from real research, covering various biological contexts including natural dy- namic changes, disturbances, additional intervention conditions, and multi-scale research targets.. Our evaluation of methods such as CoT and graph-augmented reasoning, shows that LLMs struggle with pathway reasoning, especially in per- turbed systems..
