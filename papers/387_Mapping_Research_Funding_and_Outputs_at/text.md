Gard B. Jenset, gard.jenset@springernature.com

Nature Research Intelligence, Springer Nature

### Research questions

### Methods and data

- • What is the relationship between research grants, publication numbers, and citations in the Nordic region?
- • What role does topic or field play?


- • Dimensions data (Hook et al., 2018), enriched with topic and field information (Jenset et al. 2025).
- • Lagged panel design: funding before 2020, publications 2020-2024.
- • Papers assigned to country based on correspond author affiliation.
- • Mixed effects regression model with field as random effect and logtransformed variables.
- • Correspondence Analysis.


## Results

![image 1](Jenset_2026_Mapping Research Funding and Outputs at the Topic Level in the Nordic Countries_images/imageFile1.png)

- • Strong positive correlation of grants vs. lagged publications (0.74) but weaker than overall global correlation (0.81).
- • Negligible negative correlation of funding and citations (-0.16) corroborates previous work (Öquist & Benner 2014).
- • Observed differences between countries in publications by field adds nuance to Narayan et al. (2024).
- • A regression model with ln(num. publications) as response, controlling for country and field, shows that a 1% increase in


- o Funding ~ 9.5% increase in publications
- o Citations ~ 7.4% decrease in publications
