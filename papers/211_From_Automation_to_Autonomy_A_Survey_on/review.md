# From Automation to Autonomy: A Survey on Large Language Models in Scientific Discovery
> **저자**: Tianshi Zheng et al. | **날짜**: 2025.05 | **arXiv**: [2505.13259](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.13259)

---

## Essence

This survey systematically charts this burgeoning field, placing a central focus on the changing roles and escalating capabilities of LLMs in science. Overall, this survey provides a conceptual architecture and strategic foresight to navigate and shape the future of AI-driven scientific discovery, fostering both rapid innovation and responsible advancement.

## Motivation

- **Known**: Large Language Models (LLMs) are catalyzing a paradigm shift in scientific discovery, evolving from task-specific automation tools into increasingly autonomous agents and fundamentally redefining research processes and human-AI collaboration.
- **Gap**: We further identify pivotal challenges and future research trajectories such as robotic automation, self-improvement, and ethical governance.
- **Approach**: This survey systematically charts this burgeoning field, placing a central focus on the changing roles and escalating capabilities of LLMs in science.

## Achievement

1. This survey systematically charts this burgeoning field, placing a central focus on the changing roles and escalating capabilities of LLMs in science.
2. Through the lens of the scientific method, we introduce a foundational three-level taxonomy-Tool, Analyst, and Scientist-to delineate their escalating autonomy and evolving responsibilities within the research lifecycle.
3. Overall, this survey provides a conceptual architecture and strategic foresight to navigate and shape the future of AI-driven scientific discovery, fostering both rapid innovation and responsible advancement.
