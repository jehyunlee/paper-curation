In pursuit of artificial general intelligence (AGI), there is a growing need for models that not only execute commands or retrieve information but also learn, reason, and generate new knowledge by formulat- ing novel hypotheses and theories that deepen our understanding of the world.. Guided by Peirce’s framework of abduction, deduction, and induction, this survey offers a structured lens to examine LLM-based hypothesis discovery.. We synthesize existing work in hypothesis generation, application, and validation, identifying both key achievements and critical gaps..
