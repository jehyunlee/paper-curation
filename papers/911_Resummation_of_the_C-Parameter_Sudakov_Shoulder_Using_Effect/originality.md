Abstract The C-parameter distribution in e+e−annihilation exhibits a kinematic shoulder at C = 3/4, where three-parton final states reach their maximum and a fourth parton is required to exceed it.. This boundary generates large logarithms that must be resummed.. Using soft- collinear effective theory, we derive a factorization theorem involving new jet and soft func- tions specific to the C-parameter measurement, in which soft radiation contributes quadrat- ically in transverse momentum.. This quadratic structure explains the step discontinuity at leading order.. We compute all ingredients at one loop, validate against Monte Carlo, and present matched NLL+NLO results.. Unlike thrust and heavy jet mass, the C-parameter has no Sudakov–Landau pole, making momentum-space resummation straightforward..
