Lane6 ✉ There is a well-documented gap between the observed number of works produced by women and by men in science, with clear consequences for the retention and promotion of women1.. The gap might be a result of productivity differences2–5, or it might be owing to women’s contributions not being acknowledged6,7.. Here we find that at least part of this gap is the result of unacknowledged contributions: women in research teams are significantly less likely than men to be credited with authorship.. The findings are consistent across three very different sources of data..
