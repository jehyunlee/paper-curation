We present an interactive biomedical claim verification system by integrating LLMs, transparent model explanations, and user-guided justification.. In the system, users first retrieve relevant scientific studies from a persistent medical literature corpus and explore how different LLMs perform natural language inference (NLI) within task-adaptive reasoning framework to classify each study as "Support," "Contradict," or "Not Enough Information" regarding the claim.. Users can examine the model’s reasoning process with additional insights provided by SHAP values that highlight word-level contributions to the final result.. This combination enables a more transparent and interpretable evaluation of the model’s decision-making process.. A summary stage allows users to consolidate the results by selecting a result with narrative justification generated by LLMs.
