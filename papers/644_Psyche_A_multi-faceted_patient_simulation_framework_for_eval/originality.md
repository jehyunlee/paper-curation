Since psychiatric assessments typically involve complex conversational interactions between psychiatrists and patients, there is growing interest in developing LLM-based psychiatric assessment conversational agents (PACAs) that aim to simulate the role of psychiatrists in clinical evaluations.. However, standardized methods for benchmarking the clinical appropriateness of PACAs’ interaction with patients still remain underexplored.. Here, we propose PSYCHE, a novel framework designed to enable the 1) clinically relevant, 2) ethically safe, 3) cost-efficient, and 4) quantitative evaluation of PACAs.. This is achieved by simulating psychiatric patients based on a multi-faceted psychiatric construct that defines the simulated patients’ profiles, histories, and behaviors, which PACAs are expected to assess..
