ABSTRACT Software is one of the most powerful tools that we humans have at our disposal; it allows a skilled programmer to interact with the world in complex and pro- found ways.. At the same time, thanks to improvements in large language models (LLMs), there has also been a rapid development in AI agents that interact with and affect change in their surrounding environments.. In this paper, we introduce OpenHands (f.k.a.. OpenDevin), a platform for the development of powerful and flexible AI agents that interact with the world in similar ways to those of a human developer: by writing code, interacting with a command line, and browsing the web.. We describe how the platform allows for the implementation of new agents, safe interaction with sandboxed environments for code execution, coordination between multiple agents, and incorporation of evaluation benchmarks..
