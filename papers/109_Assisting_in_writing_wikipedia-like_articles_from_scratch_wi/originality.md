Abstract We study how to apply large language models to write grounded and organized long-form ar- ticles from scratch, with comparable breadth and depth to Wikipedia pages.. This underex- plored problem poses new challenges at the pre-writing stage, including how to research the topic and prepare an outline prior to writ- ing.. We propose STORM, a writing system for the Synthesis of Topic Outlines through Retrieval and Multi-perspective Question Ask- ing.. STORM models the pre-writing stage by (1) discovering diverse perspectives in research- ing the given topic, (2) simulating conversa- tions where writers carrying different perspec- tives pose questions to a topic expert grounded on trusted Internet sources, (3) curating the col- lected information to create an outline.. For evaluation, we curate FreshWiki, a dataset of recent high-quality Wikipedia articles, and formulate outline assessments to evaluate the pre-writing stage..
