Abstract Literature reviews are an essential component of scientific research, but they remain time- intensive and challenging to write, especially due to the recent influx of research papers.. This paper explores the zero-shot abilities of recent Large Language Models (LLMs) in assisting with the writing of literature reviews based on an abstract.. We decompose the task into two components: (1) Retrieving related works given a query abstract and (2) Writing a literature review based on the retrieved results.. We analyze how effective LLMs are for both components.. For retrieval, we introduce a novel two-step search strategy that first uses an LLM to extract meaningful keywords from the abstract of a paper and then retrieves potentially relevant papers by querying an external knowledge base..
