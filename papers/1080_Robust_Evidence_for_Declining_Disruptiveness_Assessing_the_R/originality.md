Abstract We respond to Holst et al.’s critique that the decline in scientific disruptiveness documented in Park et al.. (Nature, 2023) is an artifact of including works with zero backward citations.. Using their advocated dataset, metric, and exclusion criteria, we find declines equivalent to major benchmark transformations in science.. Their own regression model—designed to address their concerns about zero-citation works— yields large and significant declines for both papers and patents (p<0.001), a result found in their sup- plementary tables yet left unaddressed, despite directly contradicting their central claim.. Their critique is further undermined by severe quality issues in their data, which contain three times more zero-citation works than ours..
