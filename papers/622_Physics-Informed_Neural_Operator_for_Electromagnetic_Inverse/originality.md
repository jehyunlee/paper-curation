Abstract—This paper proposes a physics-informed neural op- erator (PINO) framework for solving inverse scattering problems, enabling rapid and accurate reconstructions under diverse mea- surement conditions.. In the proposed approach, the dielectric property is represented as a learnable tensor, while a neural operator is employed to predict the induced current distribution.. A hybrid loss function, consisting of the state loss, data loss and total-variation (TV) regularization, is constructed to establish a fully differentiable formulation for a joint optimization of network parameters and dielectric property.. To demonstrate the framework’s generality and flexibility, PINO is implemented using three representative neural operators, i.e., the Fourier Neural Operator (FNO), the enhanced Fourier Neural Opera- tor (U-FNO) and the Factorized Fourier Neural Operator (F- FNO)..
