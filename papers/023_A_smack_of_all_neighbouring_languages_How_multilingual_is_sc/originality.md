Using two major bibliometric databases (OpenAlex and Dimensions), we provide a large-scale analysis of linguistic diversity in science, considering both the language of publications (N=87,577,942) and of cited references (N=1,480,570,087).. For the 1990-2023 period, we find that only Indonesian, Portuguese and Spanish have expanded at a faster pace than English.. Country-level analyses show that this trend is due to the growing strength of the Latin American and Indonesian academic circuits..
