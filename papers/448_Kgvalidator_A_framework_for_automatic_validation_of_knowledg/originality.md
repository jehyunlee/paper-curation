Abstract This study explores the use of Large Language Models (LLMs) for automatic evaluation of knowledge graph (KG) completion models.. Historically, validating information in KGs has been a challenging task, requiring large-scale human annotation at prohibitive cost.. With the emergence of general-purpose generative AI and LLMs, it is now plausible that human-in-the-loop validation could be replaced by a generative agent.. We introduce a framework for consistency and validation when using generative models to validate knowledge graphs.. Our framework is based upon recent open-source developments for structural and semantic validation of LLM outputs, and upon flexible approaches to fact checking and verification, supported by the capacity to reference external knowledge sources of any kind..
