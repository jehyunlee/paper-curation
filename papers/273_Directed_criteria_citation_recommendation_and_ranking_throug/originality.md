ABSTRACT We explore link prediction as a proxy for automatically surfacing documents from existing literature that might be topically or con- textually relevant to a new document.. Our model uses transformer- based graph embeddings to encode the meaning of each document, presented as a node within a citation network.. We show that the semantic representations that our model generates can outperform other content-based methods in recommendation and ranking tasks.. This provides a holistic approach to exploring citation graphs in domains where it is critical that these documents properly cite each other, so as to minimize the possibility of any inconsistencies..
