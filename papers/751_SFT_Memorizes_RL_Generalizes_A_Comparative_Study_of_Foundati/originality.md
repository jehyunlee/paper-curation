We introduce GeneralPoints, an arithmetic reasoning card game, and also con- sider V-IRL, a real-world navigation environ- ment, to assess how models trained with SFT and RL generalize to unseen variants in both novel textual rules and visual domains.. We show that RL, especially when trained with an outcome- based reward, generalizes in both the rule-based textual and visual environments.. SFT, in con- trast, tends to memorize the training data and struggles to generalize out-of-distribution in ei- ther scenario..
