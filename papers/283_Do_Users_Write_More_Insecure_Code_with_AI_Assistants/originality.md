In this paper, we conduct a user study to examine how users interact with AI code assistants to solve a variety of security related tasks.. Overall, we find that participants who had access to an AI assistant wrote significantly less secure code than those without access to an assistant.. Partici- pants with access to an AI assistant were also more likely to believe they wrote secure code, suggesting that such tools may lead users to be overconfident about security flaws in their code.. To better inform the design of future AI-based code assistants, we release our user-study apparatus and anonymized data to researchers seeking to build on our work at this link..
