Abstract We introduce Codex, a GPT language model fine- tuned on publicly available code from GitHub, and study its Python code-writing capabilities.. A distinct production version of Codex powers GitHub Copilot.. On HumanEval, a new evalua- tion set we release to measure functional correct- ness for synthesizing programs from docstrings, our model solves 28.8% of the problems, while GPT-3 solves 0% and GPT-J solves 11.4%.. Fur- thermore, we find that repeated sampling from the model is a surprisingly effective strategy for pro- ducing working solutions to difficult prompts.. Us- ing this method, we solve 70.2% of our problems with 100 samples per problem.. Careful investiga- tion of our model reveals its limitations, including difficulty with docstrings describing long chains of operations and with binding operations to vari- ables..
