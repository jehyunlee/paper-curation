In this work, we explore a comple- mentary application: using LLMs as verifiers to automate the academic verifi- cation of scientific manuscripts.. To that end, we introduce SPOT, a dataset of 83 published papers paired with 91 errors significant enough to prompt errata or retraction, cross-validated with actual authors and human annotators.. Evaluat- ing state-of-the-art LLMs on SPOT, we find that none surpasses 21.1% recall or 6.1% precision (o3 achieves the best scores, with all others near zero).. Further- more, confidence estimates are uniformly low, and across eight independent runs, models rarely rediscover the same errors, undermining their reliability..
