Inspired by real-world research dynamics, this paper inves- tigates whether structured multi-agent discussions can sur- pass solitary ideation.. We propose a cooperative multi-agent framework for generating research proposals and systemat- ically compare configurations including group size, leader- led versus leaderless structures, and team compositions vary- ing in interdisciplinarity and seniority.. To assess idea qual- ity, we employ a comprehensive protocol with agent-based scoring and human review across dimensions such as nov- elty, strategic vision, and integration depth.. Our results show that multi-agent discussions substantially outperform solitary baselines..
